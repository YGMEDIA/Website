The short answer

A professional website costs
between 1,500 and 20,000 euros.

The wide range has a reason: the price depends on who builds and what gets built. A builder costs a few euros a month, but you do everything yourself. A freelancer builds an individual business website usually for 1,500 to 5,000 euros. An agency often charges 5,000 to 20,000 euros or more for the same project, because project managers, meetings and overhead are paid along. In the end, what matters is not the price, but whether the website brings inquiries. A cheap website nobody finds is more expensive than a fair investment that generates customers.

Cost factors

What the price
really depends on.

Two websites can look the same on the outside and still be ten times apart in price. The difference is in the work you do not see: strategy, search engine optimization, clean technology and content that sells.

Once you understand which factors drive the price, you immediately recognize whether an offer is fairly calculated or whether someone is selling a template at a premium price. To see how such a website is built in practice, visit the web design page.

Scope and features

A website with 5 pages costs less than one with 20. Booking tools, multiple languages, shop integration or custom calculators increase the effort and thus the price.

02

Individual design vs. template

An adapted template is done in hours. A design that truly communicates your brand needs a concept, drafts and refinement. This is exactly where the visible quality difference is made.

03

SEO and content

Copy that sells and a structure that ranks on Google are real work. Websites without SEO are cheaper, but invisible. That is the most common reason why cheap websites bring no inquiries.

04

Hidden and ongoing costs

Hosting at 5 to 30 euros per month, the domain at 10 to 20 euros per year, plus possibly maintenance, plugin licenses and copywriting. Serious offers name these items upfront, not on the first invoice.
